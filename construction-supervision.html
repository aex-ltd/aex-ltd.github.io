Construction Supervision Division

The Construction Supervision Division consists of construction managers, site supervisors, safety officers, and quality control inspectors. Personnel typically hold degrees in construction management, civil engineering, or related fields and have certifications such as PMP (Project Management Professional), OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration) certifications, and LEED AP. The division utilizes project management software like Microsoft Project and Primavera P6 to manage schedules and resources, as well as Procore and BIM 360 for construction management and collaboration. Safety management is supported by tools like SafetyCulture (iAuditor), while quality control is ensured through software like Bluebeam Revu. Additionally, our experienced personnel in Nigeria ensure continuous, real-time coordination with international consultants, providing a direct and professional link to our clients. The role of AEX is to complete the missions by itself or with our international consultants EXP and AAA by assuring the perfect, professional, and easy coordination between the client, AEX, and our international consultants to ensure that all missions are completed to the entire satisfaction of the client. This professional team, equipped with innovative software, ensures that construction projects are executed safely, on time, and to the highest quality standards.

Coordinate with contractors to ensure corrective actions are taken. Ensure all inspections meet regulatory and industry standards.
  • Monitoring construction progress and quality : Oversee construction activities to ensure adherence to project timelines, quality standards, and safety regulations. Track progress and ensure milestones are met. Use project management software and tools to monitor progress. Report on construction status regularly. Conduct thorough inspections and reviews. Use compliance checklists and protocols. Document and address any non-compliance issues. Ensure all necessary permits and approvals are obtained before construction begins. Collaborate with regulatory authorities to ensure compliance.

Ensure compliance with health and safety regulations. Use safety management systems and tools. Conduct regular safety audits and inspections. Address any safety issues promptly to prevent accidents. Promote a culture of safety and risk management on-site.
  • Reviewing and approving contractor work and materials : Inspect and approve contractor work and materials to ensure they meet project requirements and quality standards. Maintain detailed records of approvals. Use standardized approval processes and documentation. Address any deficiencies or issues promptly. Ensure all materials meet regulatory and industry standards. Collaborate with contractors to ensure quality workmanship.
  • Coordinating with project managers and contractors : Facilitate effective communication and coordination between project managers, contractors, and other stakeholders. Resolve conflicts and ensure smooth operations. Use collaboration tools and platforms. Conduct regular coordination meetings and workshops. Ensure all stakeholders are informed and engaged. Implement solutions promptly to minimize delays. Use issue tracking and resolution tools. Document and communicate resolutions to all stakeholders.   • Maintaining detailed project documentation and records : Keep accurate and detailed records of all construction activities, inspections, and changes. Ensure documentation is up-to-date and accessible. Use project management software and document management systems. Maintain organized and comprehensive project files. Prepare detailed progress reports for review. Use meeting agendas and minutes to document discussions. Ensure all stakeholders are informed and engaged.
