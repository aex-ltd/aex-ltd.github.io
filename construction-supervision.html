Construction Supervision Division

The Construction Supervision Division at AEX includes construction managers, site supervisors, safety officers, and quality control inspectors, with degrees and certifications like PMP, OSHA, and LEED AP. They use software such as Microsoft Project, Primavera P6, Procore, BIM 360, SafetyCulture (iAuditor), and Bluebeam Revu for project management, safety, and quality control. Personnel in Nigeria ensure real-time coordination with international consultants, maintaining a professional client link. Collaborating with EXP and AAA, AEX ensures missions are completed professionally, safely, on time, and to high-quality standards.

Conducting site inspections and assessments ▶

Perform regular site inspections and assessments to monitor construction progress and ensure compliance with design specifications. Document findings and address issues promptly. Use advanced inspection techniques and tools. Maintain detailed inspection records and reports. Coordinate with contractors to ensure corrective actions are taken. Ensure all inspections meet regulatory and industry standards.

Monitoring construction progress and quality ▶

Oversee construction activities to ensure adherence to project timelines, quality standards, and safety regulations. Track progress and ensure milestones are met. Use project management software and tools to monitor progress. Report on construction status regularly. Address any delays or deviations from the plan promptly. Ensure continuous improvement in construction quality and efficiency.

Ensuring compliance with design specifications and standards ▶

Verify that construction work meets all design specifications, industry standards, and regulatory requirements. Conduct thorough inspections and reviews. Use compliance checklists and protocols. Document and address any non-compliance issues. Ensure all necessary permits and approvals are obtained before construction begins. Collaborate with regulatory authorities to ensure compliance.

Managing on-site safety and risk assessments ▶

Implement safety protocols and conduct risk assessments to maintain a safe working environment on-site. Ensure compliance with health and safety regulations. Use safety management systems and tools. Conduct regular safety audits and inspections. Address any safety issues promptly to prevent accidents. Promote a culture of safety and risk management on-site.

Reviewing and approving contractor work and materials ▶

Inspect and approve contractor work and materials to ensure they meet project requirements and quality standards. Maintain detailed records of approvals. Use standardized approval processes and documentation. Address any deficiencies or issues promptly. Ensure all materials meet regulatory and industry standards. Collaborate with contractors to ensure quality workmanship.

Coordinating with project managers and contractors ▶

Facilitate effective communication and coordination between project managers, contractors, and other stakeholders. Resolve conflicts and ensure smooth operations. Use collaboration tools and platforms. Conduct regular coordination meetings and workshops. Ensure all stakeholders are informed and engaged. Address any coordination issues promptly to maintain project schedules.

Identifying and resolving construction issues and conflicts ▶

Address and resolve any construction-related issues or conflicts that arise during the project. Implement solutions promptly to minimize delays. Use issue tracking and resolution tools. Document and communicate resolutions to all stakeholders. Collaborate with contractors and project managers to address issues. Ensure continuous improvement in issue resolution processes.

Maintaining detailed project documentation and records ▶

Keep accurate and detailed records of all construction activities, inspections, and changes. Ensure documentation is up-to-date and accessible. Use project management software and document management systems. Maintain organized and comprehensive project files. Ensure all documentation meets regulatory and industry standards. Provide regular updates and reports to stakeholders.

Conducting regular site meetings and progress reports ▶

Hold regular site meetings to review progress, address issues, and provide updates to stakeholders. Prepare detailed progress reports for review. Use meeting agendas and minutes to document discussions. Ensure all stakeholders are informed and engaged. Address any issues raised during meetings promptly. Ensure continuous improvement in meeting and reporting processes.

Ensuring adherence to project schedules and timelines ▶

Monitor and manage project schedules to ensure timely completion of construction activities. Adjust plans as necessary to meet deadlines. Use project scheduling tools and techniques. Communicate any schedule changes to all stakeholders promptly. Address any delays or deviations from the plan promptly. Ensure continuous improvement in project scheduling and time management.

Overseeing testing and commissioning of systems ▶

Supervise the testing and commissioning of mechanical, electrical, and other systems to ensure proper functionality. Verify system performance and compliance. Use testing and commissioning protocols and checklists. Document and address any issues identified during testing. Collaborate with contractors and system specialists to ensure successful commissioning. Ensure continuous improvement in testing and commissioning processes.

Providing final inspections and project closeout documentation ▶

Conduct final inspections and prepare closeout documentation to confirm project completion and compliance. Ensure all punch list items are addressed. Use standardized closeout procedures and documentation. Provide clients with comprehensive project completion reports. Address any remaining issues promptly to ensure client satisfaction. Ensure continuous improvement in project closeout processes.
